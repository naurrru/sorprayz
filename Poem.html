Stars, a love poem

by Emmanuel Metrillo

Such beauty in the stars I can only imagine,

A cosmic canvas, where wonders align.

Each star may sparkle, with brilliance so high,

Yet none can compare to the gleam in her eye.


I gaze at Orion, with its mighty allure,

But her eyes hold galaxies, vast and pure.

The Milky Way cascades, a river of light,

Yet her gaze outshines, in the still of the night.


Amongst the stardust, I search in awe,

For a sparkle as captivating, a celestial law.

But none can rival her eyes' tender grace,

In their warmth, I find my sacred space.


Her giggles, like chimes in a gentle breeze,

Bring joy to my heart, put my soul at ease.

Each laugh, a melody, so sweet and pure,

It resonates in the cosmos, a celestial allure.


Her pale white skin, a canvas so divine,

A masterpiece crafted by stars' design.

In the moon's soft glow, it glimmers and gleams,

A reflection of galaxies, in her earthly dreams.


And the warmth of her soul, like the sun's embrace,

Radiates love, filling every space.

In her tender touch, I find solace and light,

Guiding me through the darkest of night.


So, let the stars twinkle in their celestial dance,

For in her eyes, I find my true romance.

In the vastness of space, where beauty lies,

Her eyes are the stars that light up my skies.
